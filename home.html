About This Service

Dedicated anglers know that a river’s condition at any given time can make or break the chances of making a catch. However, gauging a river’s streamflow and temperature is often easier said than done, especially when you’re not already on site. This project aims to provide reliable, real-time data to simplify the process of assessing a river’s conditions. Gathered from the United States Geological Survey (USGS), the data points accurately represent streamflow rates of over 140 sites in Utah.

At the moment, temperature collection is limited to just a few sites. Fortunately, streamflow is generally regarded as the more valuable measurement when it comes to assessing fly fishing conditions.

Key Features

Instant River Insights
Real-time data gives an accurate idea of a river's condition at any moment.
Effortless Collection Management
Users can save collections of rivers for easy monitoring. To add a group of rivers to a collection and view them from the Home page, just drag the rivers into the specified area, give the collection a name, and click “save.”
Data Interpretation Tips
Rivertrack offers tips on the explore page to help users interpret and apply the data.
